Setting Up OneDrive on your Window's Computer

Why Do Backups?

As a system’s administrator, one of the most important considerations is to backup all your data and configurations. The loss of data is devastating and results in long hours to rebuild the service and leaves the possibility of angry users calling who depend on the data. Backups from the user perspective is just as important but setting up a backup solution isn’t a concern until after data is lost. When asked by friends and family if it was possible to grab some important file their system that’s completely broke, I always ask if the user did a backup but receive the same answer, No. This is my solution to how I backup my files to the cloud and created a roaming profile that synchronizes my data on all my systems. Note that it's also important to keep a local backup as ransomware attacks could make it impossible to access your data.

Why OneDrive?

OneDrive is the system that I use. You can use Dropbox, Google Drive, ICloud or other user storage solutions.

OneDrive Requirements

Requirements
An active Microsoft Account
Microsoft OneDrive Install on the Operating System
Network Connection
Windows OS: 32-bit or 64-bit version of Windows 10, Windows 8/8.1, or Windows 7
Processor: 1.6 GHz or higher, or Intel-based Mac computer, or a Windows 10 ARM-based PC
Memory: 1 GB of RAM or higher

Open the OneDrive Setup

Once Microsoft OneDrive is installed on your system, run the OneDrive setup by searching “OneDrive” In the Window’s search bar and left clicking on the “OneDrive” app that is shown in the search results.

Open OneDrive Settings

On the OneDrive setup screen, type in the email used for your Mircosoft Account and click on the "Sign in" Button. On the next window, type in your password for your account. Note that a Microsoft Account is required for settting up OneDrive.

Your OneDrive folder Location

For your OneDrive folder, select where you want your OneDrive folder to be located on your system. By default, your OneDrive folder will be located in your user directory on your C drive. Click next after selecting your location.

Back up your folders

On the “Back up your folder” window, make sure all three folders are selected: Desktop, Documents, and Pictures. By selecting these options, all items placed in these locations are synced to your OneDrive folder. Click Continue.

Getting to know your OneDrive

Your OneDrive setup is now complete. The next few windows are there to teach you about how OneDrive functions.
